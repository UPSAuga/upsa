About UPSA

What is UPSA?

The Uganda Pharmaceutical Students Association (UPSA) is the national body representing all pharmaceutical students in Uganda. It serves as a platform for collaboration, professional development, and advocacy within the pharmaceutical education sector.

The Association is a national non-partisan, non-religious, non-profit umbrella body uniting pharmacy students and recent pharmacy graduates in Uganda.

Who is UPSA?

The Uganda Pharmaceutical Students’ Association (UPSA) is the national umbrella body that unites and represents all pharmacy students in Uganda. Established to promote academic excellence, professional development, and collaboration among future pharmacists, UPSA serves as a central platform for advocacy, networking, and innovation within the pharmaceutical student community. Currently, UPSA is composed of three mother associations from the leading pharmacy training institutions in Uganda:

KIUPSA

Kampala International University

MUPSA

Makerere University

MBUPSA

Mbarara University

History

The Association was founded in 2012, and later revived to functionality in 2016 by representatives of three pharmacy school students’ Associations i.e. Kampala International University Pharmacy Students’ Association (KIUPSA), Makerere University Pharmacy Students’ Association (MUPSA), and Mbarara University Pharmacy Students’ Association (MBUPSA

The association has successfully organized numerous national conferences, medical outreach programs, and advocacy campaigns, significantly contributing to the development of pharmacy education in Uganda.

Vision

To be the leading pharmacy student’s organisation in the World representing its members and advancing the profession of Pharmacy.

Mission

The Mission of the Association is; to promote unity among pharmacy students and its members nationally through advocacy and professional development in nurturing a holistic pharmacist.

The Core Values of the Association is below following in form of the acronym UPSA.

  • Unity
  • Professionalism
  • Sociability
  • Accountability

Objectives

  • Promote unity amongst all pharmacy students and its members nationally, and establish and/or maintain ties with other organisations having related or similar objects.
  • Contribute to the promotion and maintenance of good health through advocacy for health policy change and the provision of pharmaceutical services to the communities in Uganda.
  • Promote and participate in research and innovations in the field of pharmacy.
  • Contribute to the promotion of proper drug usage and safety.
  • Avail a voice and channel through which challenges in pharmacy practice and pharmacy education can be discussed and call for reforms relating to that.
  • Advocate for, promote, protect the interests and provide services for the wellbeing of its members in relation to the study and practice of the profession of pharmacy.
  • Uphold and nurture professional integrity and standards of Pharmacy Students’ Code of Conduct.

What is MedXMentor?

The program aims to bridge the gap between academic training and real-world pharmacy practice through personalized guidance, skill-building workshops, and career support.

UPSA Events

Explore our impactful events that bring together pharmaceutical students, professionals, and communities to advance healthcare and education.

Medical Camp

Our annual Medical Camp is a flagship outreach program that provides free healthcare services to underserved communities across Uganda. Students from all member associations participate in this initiative, gaining practical experience while making a difference.

The camp typically lasts for a week and includes general consultations, basic laboratory tests, medication distribution, and health education sessions. It's an opportunity for students to apply their knowledge in real-world settings and develop essential clinical skills.

In 2024, we successfully conducted medical camps in three districts: Nakaseke, Katakwi, and Sironko, serving over 2,000 patients and distributing essential medications to those in need.

UPSA Symposium

The UPSA Symposium is our premier academic event that brings together pharmaceutical students, educators, and industry professionals to discuss current trends, challenges, and innovations in pharmacy practice.

Held annually, the symposium features keynote speeches, panel discussions, workshops, and presentations covering various aspects of pharmaceutical sciences and practice. It provides a platform for students to showcase research, exchange ideas, and network with professionals in the field.

The 2024 symposium focused on "Innovations in Pharmaceutical Care" and attracted over 500 participants from all pharmacy schools in Uganda and beyond. Notable speakers included industry leaders, government officials, and international experts in pharmaceutical sciences.

We welcome contributions from students, faculty, and professionals in the pharmaceutical field. To submit an article for publication on the UPSA website:

  2. Include a short author bio and relevant images (if available)

The Future of Pharmacy Practice in Uganda

J
By Jane Doe May 15, 2025

Pharmacy practice in Uganda is undergoing a significant transformation driven by technological advancements, policy changes, and evolving healthcare needs. As the country strives to achieve universal health coverage, the role of pharmacists is expanding beyond traditional dispensing functions to include clinical services, patient counseling, and public health initiatives.

Technological Advancements

The integration of digital technologies in pharmacy practice is revolutionizing how medications are managed and dispensed. Electronic prescribing systems, inventory management software, and telepharmacy services are being gradually adopted across urban centers. These technologies not only improve efficiency but also reduce medication errors and enhance patient safety.

In rural areas, mobile health (mHealth) applications are bridging the gap between patients and healthcare providers. Pharmacists are using smartphones to conduct remote consultations, monitor chronic conditions, and provide medication adherence support to patients in remote locations.

Policy and Regulatory Changes

The National Drug Authority (NDA) has recently introduced several regulatory reforms aimed at improving pharmaceutical services. These include stricter licensing requirements for pharmacies, enhanced monitoring of drug quality, and expanded scope of practice for pharmacists. The new guidelines now allow pharmacists to initiate certain treatments for minor ailments and provide vaccinations, aligning Uganda with international best practices.

Additionally, the Ministry of Health has developed a National Pharmaceutical Policy that emphasizes the importance of pharmaceutical care and rational use of medicines. This policy framework provides a roadmap for strengthening pharmaceutical services across all levels of the healthcare system.

Education and Training

Pharmacy education in Uganda is also evolving to meet the changing demands of the profession. The curriculum in accredited pharmacy schools now includes more clinical training, communication skills, and public health components. Interprofessional education is being promoted to foster collaboration between pharmacists and other healthcare providers.

Continuing professional development programs are being established to ensure that practicing pharmacists stay current with the latest advances in pharmacotherapy and healthcare delivery. These programs are crucial for maintaining high standards of practice and ensuring patient safety.

Challenges and Opportunities

Despite these positive developments, several challenges remain. There is still a shortage of pharmacists, particularly in rural areas, and infrastructure limitations hinder the implementation of advanced pharmaceutical services. Supply chain issues continue to affect the availability of essential medicines, and funding for pharmaceutical services remains inadequate.

However, these challenges also present opportunities for innovation and improvement. Public-private partnerships are emerging as a viable model for expanding pharmaceutical services. Community pharmacy initiatives are gaining traction, and there is growing recognition of the value pharmacists bring to the healthcare system.

The Road Ahead

The future of pharmacy practice in Uganda is promising. With continued investment in education, infrastructure, and policy development, pharmacists are poised to play a more central role in healthcare delivery. The Uganda Pharmaceutical Students Association (UPSA) is actively involved in advocating for the profession and preparing the next generation of pharmacists to meet the challenges and opportunities ahead.

As we look to the future, it is clear that pharmacy practice in Uganda will continue to evolve, driven by innovation, collaboration, and a shared commitment to improving health outcomes for all Ugandans.
